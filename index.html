Common leopard gecko

Unlike most geckos, all species in the genus possess movable eyelids, and cannot climb up smooth surfaces.

  • Binomial name: Eublepharis macularius
  • Habitat: south-Asian Afghanistan, Pakistan, north-west India, and some parts of Iran
  • Diet: crickets, roaches, mealworms/super worms
  • Length: 18 to 28 cm

The common leopard gecko or spotted fat-tailed gecko is a cathemeral, ground-dwelling lizard that have the ability to voluntarily detach their tails if it is attacked or exposed to life danger. The tail will start to regenerate immediately because it is needed for survival. This is called caudal autotomy.
